A Three-Module Offering Architecture

A clear process to take clients from diagnostic to full-scale industrialisation.

01

CORE Value Discovery for Energy AI

Objective

Start from the client’s current situation and map existing and potential AI use cases across two to three core business processes.

Content

  • Analysis of current AI and data initiatives.
  • Targeted interviews with Business, Data, IT and Risk.
  • Use of CORE to link data, decisions and actions.

Deliverables

  • Portfolio of AI use cases prioritised by value/feasibility.
  • High level business case for each use case.
  • Shortlist of one or two flagship use cases.
02

Detailed design and AI governance

Objective

Design in detail two flagship use cases and define the AI operating model and governance.

Content

  • Deeper CORE work on target processes (journeys, rules, KPIs).
  • Definition of AI governance: roles and responsibilities.
  • Structuring of the AI portfolio with SmartPMO.

Deliverables

  • Detailed blueprints for flagship use cases.
  • AI governance model and RACI.
  • A 12 to 18 month roadmap for implementation.
03

Run support and replication

Objective

Secure the first months of run and prepare replication to other processes, countries or BUs.

Content

  • Support the client in steering the programme.
  • Formalise value tracking, risk and incident management.
  • Capitalise and build a replication playbook.

Deliverables

  • Operational governance routine for the AI programme.
  • Documented lessons learned & impact measurement.
  • Internal replication playbook for the client.

2026 Objective: Sell at least one Module 1+2 combination, then use this "lighthouse" to propose Module 3 and extend the offering to other accounts.

Focus Use Case 1

AI Demand & RES Forecasting

At a Glance

Core process for portfolio steering, PPA sizing, and hedging.

Value5 / 5
Maturity4 / 5

01. Client Stakes & Maturity

  • For utilities, IPPs and some funds, the ability to forecast demand and renewable generation directly conditions investment, PPA and flexibility decisions.
  • Forecast errors translate into curtailment, balancing costs, under-utilisation of assets or poorly calibrated contracts.
  • On the technical side, robust AI models already exist for load and wind or solar forecasting, supported by rapid progress in AI weather forecasting and computing power.

02. Typical CVA Proposition

  • Build with the client a single forecasting backbone combining demand, weather and renewable output, connected to portfolio and PPA decision models.
  • Use CORE to map the current data, models, decisions and actions end-to-end and identify breaks that prevent effective use of forecasts.
  • Propose a Module 1 diagnostic based on CORE and value sizing, followed by a Module 2 on detailed design and AI governance, then a lighthouse implementation.

03. Interest for the Energy Practice

  • This is a very COMEX-friendly use case with a clear link to portfolio, PPA, storage and flexibility decisions.
  • It is strongly differentiating for CVA because it brings together investment strategy, risk management, markets and AI.
  • Replication potential is high across multiple accounts in Europe and the Middle East, with a natural link to our work on networks, data centres and Energy to Compute.

Focus Use Case 3

AI in Customer Operations & Collections

At a Glance

Core processes for customer service, billing queries, complaints, and credit or collections.

Value3 / 5
Maturity4 / 5

01. Client Stakes & Maturity

  • Energy suppliers handle large volumes of calls and emails on billing, contracts, complaints and payment difficulties, under strong cost and customer satisfaction pressure.
  • Customer functions have started to test chatbots and AI assistants, but often struggle to define a clear industrialisation path and to articulate AI and human work.
  • Technical maturity is relatively high, with public examples in Energy where AI already handles a significant share of interactions.

02. Typical CVA Proposition

  • Run a CORE diagnostic of customer journeys and collections to quantify volumes, pain points, costs, satisfaction and value leakage.
  • Redesign target journeys that combine AI and human agents, with automated and AI-assisted steps, and clear rules for handover and supervision.
  • Structure a two-step programme: a lighthouse on one or two journeys, followed by a roadmap to extend to other journeys and more advanced AI use cases.

03. Interest for the Energy Practice

  • This use case is immediately understandable for customer, finance and regulatory functions, with typically faster ROI.
  • CORE-based approaches and assets can be reused across countries, which makes it a strong candidate for a repeatable CVA “product”.
  • It creates natural synergies with other commercial and digital transformation projects we already run, which makes upsell easier.
