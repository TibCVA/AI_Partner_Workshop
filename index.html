Focus programme A

Nuclear & Critical Asset Health Cockpit.

At a glance

Clients

EDF Nuclear, Framatome, Orano, Naval Group, TAQA, SEC.

Core processes

Asset strategy, life extension programmes, outage planning, capex allocation at fleet level.

01. Business problem to solve

  • Ageing nuclear and conventional fleets create a structural tension between maximising availability, ensuring safety and controlling maintenance and capex.
  • Each client has local AI and digital twin initiatives, but no integrated asset health view that drives fleet‑level decisions on life extension, major capex and outage scope.
  • Boards and regulators increasingly expect a transparent, risk‑based, data‑driven justification of these decisions.

02. What the “Cockpit” would do

  • Consolidate asset health: Create a standard health index and risk score per asset and per fleet, using sensor data, inspection results and digital twins.
  • Support LTO and capex decisions: Simulate the impact of LTO options and upgrade packages on risk, availability and cost at fleet level.
  • Structure outage scope: Propose optimised outage scopes and timings that balance risk reduction and lost generation, across multiple units.
  • Provide a COMEX‑ready view: Allow senior decision makers to compare scenarios and document decisions for regulators and owners.

03. CORE® & SmartPMO® programme structure

  • Phase 1 – CORE® diagnostic at one client: Map current asset health and LTO decision processes. Identify where existing AI and digital twins do not feed high‑level decisions. Quantify value at stake.
  • Phase 2 – Target operating model and governance: Design the standard health index, decision rules and governance. Define interfaces between engineering, operations, finance, risk and digital teams.
  • Phase 3 – Lighthouse and replication: Run a lighthouse on one fleet segment or reactor island class. Use SmartPMO® to track benefits and prepare a replication plan to other units and to clients such as Naval, TAQA or SEC.

Why this is a Wave 1 priority

  • It is directly portable across the EDF ecosystem, Naval’s fleets and large fleet owners like TAQA and SEC.
  • It positions CVA on a board‑level, safety‑critical agenda where AI is an enabler rather than the core story.
  • It leverages existing AI investments by focusing on decision design and governance, which is our core business as a strategy firm.

Focus programme B

Smart Gas & Power Network Intelligence.

At a glance

Clients

GRDF, SEC, EDF CST, TAQA.

Core processes

Network operations, safety management, renewal and reinforcement planning, dialogue with regulators.

01. Business problem to solve

  • Networks are under pressure with growing demand and integration of new sources (biomethane, PV, EV, massive air conditioning loads).
  • There are high safety and service continuity requirements, with capex envelopes under constraint and strong expectations from regulators.
  • Smart meters and sensors have been deployed, and AI has been trialled in specific areas, but there is still no complete end‑to‑end chain from anomaly detection to renewal and reinforcement decisions.

02. What a “Network Intelligence Office” would do

  • Advanced continuous monitoring: Use AI to detect in near real time anomalies in flow, pressure, current or voltage that indicate leaks, losses or faults.
  • Risk scoring on assets: Build probabilities and criticality scores for network sections or key equipment, updated continuously.
  • Risk‑based renewal planning: Prioritise replacements and reinforcements under budget constraints to maximise risk and loss reduction.
  • Steering and regulator dialogue: Provide a cockpit that links decisions to metrics understandable by regulators and territories.

03. CORE® & SmartPMO® programme structure

  • Phase 1 – “From data to decisions” diagnostic: For an anchor client (GRDF or SEC), map how smart meter and SCADA data is used today. Identify breaks in the chain between detection, field intervention and capex decisions. Quantify value at stake.
  • Phase 2 – Network Intelligence Office design: Define the organisation, processes and decision rituals. Define KPIs, alert thresholds and interactions with field teams, call centres and regulators. Write functional specs for AI tools.
  • Phase 3 – Geographical lighthouse & scale‑up: Launch a lighthouse on a limited perimeter (one urban region or asset class). Install light SmartPMO® governance to track results and prepare for larger‑scale deployment.

Why this is a Wave 1 priority

  • It responds to a core business and regulatory challenge for GRDF and SEC, directly linked to their licence to operate.
  • It allows leveraging existing smart meter and IoT investments by creating a structured decision process that is still missing.
  • It is replicable across network clients and opens the door to extensions such as connection request triage and backlog management.

Methodological backbone

CORE® and DIDA®: our reference framework.

CORE® and DIDA® give us a rigorous way to connect journeys, data, processes and AI.

What CORE® is: a proven methodology

An integrated methodology on three levels

  • User and client journeys: Design target journeys from the user’s point of view with VoC and service standards.
  • Data flows: Optimise the data model needed to steer satisfaction, cost and carbon end‑to‑end.
  • Processes: Rebuild processes in a “data‑native” way, rethinking decisions and embedding AI where it creates value.

Organised in two lots and five modules

  • Lot 1 – Baseline and redesign (data‑native):
    • M1 – E2E diagnostic and baselines (satisfaction, efficiency, carbon).
    • M2 – Target journeys.
    • M3 – Target DIDA® flows.
  • Lot 2 – Gap analysis and transformation path:
    • M4 – Process impacts and levers.
    • M5 – Transformation trajectory and base camps.

What DIDA® is: the bridge between business and AI

DIDA® is the core protocol that connects journeys, data and processes. It decomposes each journey step into four elements, forcing clarity on how AI changes decisions and actions.

D – Data

List the data needed to monitor and deliver the journey step, and identify and collect all accessible sources automatically.

I – Insights

Use analytics and AI to transform raw data into insights that are truly useful for decision‑making.

D – Decision

Define how decisions will be taken (rules, models, exceptions) based on these insights, including the role of AI.

A – Action

Build a process and, where relevant, automation that executes the decision or augments an employee to act effectively.

Why this matters: the main AI programmes (asset health, networks, asset sourcing) are process‑heavy. CORE® and DIDA® bring a rigorous, battle‑tested way to map these processes, identify the roles of data and AI, and design target, AI‑enabled processes that teams can implement.
