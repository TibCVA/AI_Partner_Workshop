Focus Programme A

Nuclear & Critical Asset Health Cockpit

At a Glance

Clients:

EDF Nuclear, Framatome, Orano, Naval Group, TAQA, SEC

Core Processes:

Asset strategy, life extension programmes, outage planning, capex allocation at fleet level.

01. Business problem to solve

  • Ageing nuclear and conventional fleets create a structural tension between maximising availability, ensuring safety, and controlling maintenance/capex.
  • Each client has local AI and digital twin initiatives, but no integrated asset health view that drives *fleet-level* decisions on life extension (LTO), major capex, and outage scope.
  • Boards and regulators increasingly expect a transparent, risk-based, data-driven justification of these decisions.

02. What the “Cockpit” would do

  • Consolidate asset health: Create a standard health index and risk score per asset/fleet, using sensor data, inspection results, and digital twins.
  • Support LTO/capex decisions: Simulate the impact of LTO options and upgrade packages on risk, availability, and cost.
  • Structure outage scope: Propose optimised outage scopes and timings that balance risk and lost generation, at fleet level.
  • Provide a COMEX-ready view: Allow senior decision-makers to compare scenarios and document decisions for regulators.

03. CORE & SmartPMO Programme Structure

  • Phase 1 – CORE Diagnostic (at one client): Map current asset health and LTO decision processes. Identify where existing AI/twins do not feed high-level decisions. Quantify value.
  • Phase 2 – Target Operating Model: Design the standard health index, decision rules, and governance. Define interfaces between engineering, ops, finance, risk, and digital.
  • Phase 3 – Lighthouse & Replication: Run a lighthouse on one fleet segment. Use SmartPMO to track benefits. Prepare a replication plan to other units and clients (TAQA, SEC).

Why this is a Wave 1 Priority

  • It is directly portable across the EDF ecosystem, Naval’s fleets, and large fleet owners like TAQA and SEC.
  • It positions CVA on a board-level, safety-critical agenda where AI is an enabler, not the core story.
  • It leverages existing AI investments by focusing on decision design and governance, which is *notre cœur de métier*.

Focus Programme B

Smart Gas & Power Network Intelligence

At a Glance

Clients:

GRDF, SEC, EDF CST, TAQA

Core Processes:

Network operations, safety management, renewal and reinforcement planning, dialogue with regulators.

01. Business problem to solve

  • Networks are under pressure with growing demand and integration of new sources (biomethane, PV, EV, massive A/C).
  • High safety and service continuity requirements, with capex envelopes under constraint and high expectations from regulators.
  • They have deployed smart meters and sensors, trialled AI in specific areas, but still lack a complete end-to-end chain from anomaly detection to renewal and reinforcement decisions.

02. What a “Network Intelligence Office” would do

  • Advanced continuous monitoring: Use AI to detect (in near real-time) anomalies in flow, pressure, current, or voltage that indicate leaks, losses, or faults.
  • Risk scoring on assets: Build probability and criticality scores for network sections or key equipment.
  • Risk-based renewal planning: Prioritise replacements and reinforcements under budget constraints to maximise risk and loss reduction.
  • Steering & regulator dialogue: Provide a cockpit that links decisions to metrics understandable by regulators and territories.

03. CORE & SmartPMO Programme Structure

  • Phase 1 – "From data to decisions" Diagnostic: For an anchor client (GRDF or SEC), map how smart meter/SCADA data is used today. Identify breaks in the chain (detection -> intervention -> capex). Quantify value at stake.
  • Phase 2 – Network Intelligence Office Design: Define the organisation, processes, and decision rituals. Define KPIs, alert thresholds, and interactions with field teams and regulators. Write functional specs for AI tools.
  • Phase 3 – Geographical Lighthouse & Scale-up: Launch a lighthouse on a limited perimeter (e.g., one urban region). Install light SmartPMO governance to track results and prepare for large-scale deployment.

Why this is a Wave 1 Priority

  • It responds to a core business and regulatory challenge for GRDF and SEC, directly linked to their license to operate.
  • It allows leveraging existing smart meter and IoT investments by creating a structured decision process that is still missing.
  • It is replicable across network clients and opens the door to extensions (e.g., connection request triage).

CORE® and DIDA®

Our reference framework for AI-enabled process redesign.

What CORE® is: A Proven Methodology

An integrated methodology on three levels

  • User / client journeys: Design target journeys from the user’s point of view (VoC, service standards).
  • Data flows: Optimise the data model needed to steer satisfaction, cost and carbon end-to-end.
  • Processes: Rebuild processes in a “data-native” way, rethinking decisions and embedding AI where it creates value.

Organised in 2 Lots and 5 Modules

  • Lot 1 – Baseline and redesign (data-native):
    • M1 – E2E diagnostic and baselines (satisfaction, efficiency, carbon).
    • M2 – Target journeys.
    • M3 – Target DIDA® flows.
  • Lot 2 – Gap analysis and transformation path:
    • M4 – Process impacts and levers.
    • M5 – Transformation trajectory and “base camps”.

What DIDA® is: The Bridge Between Business and AI

DIDA® is the core protocol that connects journeys, data, and processes. It decomposes each journey step into four elements, forcing clarity on how AI changes decisions and actions.

D – Data

List the data needed to monitor and deliver the journey step; identify and collect all accessible sources automatically.

I – Insights

Use analytics and AI to transform raw data into insights that are truly useful for decision-making.

D – Decision

Define how decisions will be taken (rules, models, exceptions) based on these insights.

A – Action

Build a process and, where relevant, automation that executes the decision or augments an employee to act effectively.

Why this matters for an AI for Energy programme: The two AI programmes (networks, asset health) are process-heavy. CORE® and DIDA® bring a rigorous, battle-tested way to map these processes, identify the exact roles of data and AI, and design target, AI-enabled processes that teams can implement.
